The origin of Soap

The first cleaner that man used was water. Prehistoric settlements were built near water to have direct access to it.

According to Greek mythology, soap originates from ancient Greece, specifically from Lesbos. On this island they sacrificed animals to the goddess Artemis. The rains washed away animal remains and burnt fats, together with the ashes from the wood of the fire, thus forming a yellow stream that ended up in the river.

When housewives did their laundry they found that when the water turned yellow the clothes cleaned better. The soap was named in honor of the poetess Sapphos who lived at that time in Lesbos and is a paraphrase of her name.
